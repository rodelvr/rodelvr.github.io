Hi, I'm Rodel.

Currently, I am working as a Technical Team Lead (Tech Lead) and Data Scientist at Adyen. I am responsible for leading a multidisciplinary team of Data Scientists/Engineers and Software Engineers that aim to produce end-to-end Machine Learning based solutions. Given my past experience, I furthermore have obtained broad knowledge on usage and implementation of business intelligence applications and data engineering pipelines.

Chances are high that somewhere in the past Adyen has made your payment happen. Adyen processes payments for Uber, Spotify, Netflix, Nike, Google, Apple, etc. Adyen is known for building a modern payment infrastructure which is directly connected to card networks and local payment methods across the world, allowing for unified commerce and providing shopper data insights to merchants. The Adyen platform enables merchants, world-wide, to accept payments in a single system, enabling revenue growth online, on mobile devices and at the point of sale.

Examples of achieved projects at Adyen:
  • A reinforcement learning framework aimed at optimizing the payment conversion rate. Several applications of this framework are packaged in a product called RevenueAccelerate. The combined revenue increase for Adyen merchants is in the hundred millions of euros...
  • A machine learning based solution for automatically recovering subscription based payments called AutoRescue. Did you ever not have enough funds on your card or bank account to make a subscription payment? AutoRescue enables merchants to recover these payments by intelligently rescheduling it at a later date.
  • Establishing and maintaining data pre-processing and data pipelines (ETL) using Apache Spark and Apache Airflow. This work has laid the building blocks of what now is the Big Data Platform of Adyen. The platform enables users to produce business intelligence insights, machine learning models and data pipelines.

My career started as a Data Scientist at ING Bank in the Netherlands. There I worked on implementing, improving and validating machine learning models for risk management. The projects focused on predictive analytics for client behaviour in mortgages and saving accounts using machine learning models. Examples of this are prediction of mortgage prepayments, savings balances and segmentation of mortgage clients based on prepayment behaviour.
